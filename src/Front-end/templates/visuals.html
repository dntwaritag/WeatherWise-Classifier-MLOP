Weather Data Insights

Explore comprehensive visualizations of weather patterns, model performance, and feature importance to better understand our prediction system.

Weather Class Distribution

Distribution of different weather conditions in our dataset

This visualization shows the balance between different weather conditions in our training dataset. A balanced dataset helps ensure our model doesn't become biased toward more common weather types.

The distribution shows that rainy days are most common in our Seattle dataset, followed by sunny days, with fog and drizzle being relatively rare.

Feature Correlation Matrix

Relationships between different weather features

This heatmap shows how different weather features correlate with each other. Strong positive correlations (darker blue) indicate features that vary together, while negative correlations (darker red) show inverse relationships.

We can see that maximum and minimum temperatures are strongly correlated, while precipitation shows negative correlation with temperature features.

Feature Importance

Which features most influence our predictions

This chart shows which weather features contribute most to our model's predictions. Features are ranked by their Gini importance, which measures how much each feature reduces impurity in the decision trees.

Maximum temperature and precipitation emerge as the most important predictors, while wind speed has relatively less impact on our model's decisions.

Seasonal Weather Trends

How weather patterns change throughout the year

This visualization shows how different weather conditions vary by month in our dataset. Each line represents the frequency of a particular weather type throughout the year.

We can clearly see seasonal patterns - sunny days peak in summer months, while rainy days are most common in winter. Snow is almost exclusively a winter phenomenon in this dataset.
